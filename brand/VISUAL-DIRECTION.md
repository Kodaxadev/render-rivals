# Render Rivals Visual Direction Working Brief

**Status:** Exploratory brand and product-design guidance  
**Product authority:** `docs/PRODUCT-UI-SCENE-PLAN.md` and `docs/MVP-VERTICAL-SLICE.md`

## Positioning

Render Rivals is not an AI website generator in the MVP and not a universal subjective design assistant. It is a local-first comparison harness that accepts independently prepared Contenders, captures equivalent evidence, applies deterministic Gates, produces a cited Recommendation, and requires a deliberate User Decision before candidate Promotion.

The visual system must not imply that Render Rivals automatically generates Contenders, objectively proves taste, or merges/deploys a “winner.”

## Visual personality

- Comparative, not combative.
- Analytical, not clinical.
- Technical, not cryptic.
- Premium, not ornamental.
- Confident, not absolute.
- Energetic in public storytelling; quiet and legible inside the product.

## Semantic color roles

The final palette remains open. Product colors are semantic and are not permanent brand judgments.

- **Current implementation:** stable neutral/indigo treatment.
- **Contender:** distinct but equal-status accent before Recommendation.
- **Candidate A/B:** anonymous accessible pair when order-blinding is active.
- **Eligible/verified:** positive semantic treatment after Gate verification.
- **Ineligible/invalid:** warning/error treatment with icon/text, never color alone.
- **Recommended:** emphasized only after deterministic policy creates Recommendation.
- **Decision accepted:** separate human-authority state.
- **Promotion completed:** operational completion state, not “victory.”
- **Evidence/canvas:** quiet neutral surfaces, grids, annotations, and citations.

Do not use green to identify the Contender before it is verified/recommended, and do not make red/green the only distinction.

## Logo criteria

The final mark should:

- work at 16, 20, 24, 32, and 48 px;
- remain recognizable in one color;
- work as square app/favicon/tray/CLI icon and horizontal wordmark;
- imply paired implementations, comparison, convergence, evidence, or deliberate selection;
- avoid esports, betting, cryptocurrency, automotive, fashion, sports-team, or game-studio resemblance;
- avoid swords, trophies, crowns, shields, lightning bolts, arena brackets, and generic AI gradients;
- remain neutral before an outcome;
- pair with a restrained durable wordmark;
- be redrawn as original vector and screened for trademark conflicts.

## Active double-R direction

A substantially original double-R is now an active exploration.

It should not be two ordinary `R` glyphs placed side by side. Explore:

- shared negative space;
- two paths entering the same measurement frame;
- mirrored but non-identical construction;
- one structural system with two states;
- paired rails, cuts, or evidence markers;
- forms that remain distinct in monochrome and tiny sizes.

Avoid a mark that reads primarily as a racing, Rolls-Royce-like, sports, luxury-fashion, crypto, or esports emblem.

## Product UI

MVP priorities:

- side-by-side matched state/viewport/interaction-step comparison;
- persistent Current/Contender or anonymous A/B labels;
- cited Factor Evidence and confidence/limitations;
- visible phased Gates and validity/invalidation;
- distinct Recommendation, User Decision, Promotion, and Export Operation states;
- candidate-local failure distinct from full Capture-Epoch invalidation;
- restrained motion limited to state change and comparison assistance;
- readable compact laptop layout and keyboard/screen-reader support.

Simple overlay is P1/conditional. Difference, flicker, annotation, rounds, and score-delta dashboards are post-MVP and must not shape the initial visual system as if shipped.

Avoid presenting one aggregate score as the visual centerpiece.

## Public storytelling

Marketing may use stronger duality than the application while remaining accurate:

- paired light fields;
- split frames with identical framing;
- state/viewport/interaction matrices;
- Evidence callouts and citations;
- Gate-failure/no-improvement/tie/invalid examples;
- current-to-Contender comparison without automatic winner language;
- before/after assets generated from real completed Runs.

Do not use arena brackets, champion/challenger storytelling, fake product screens, or unimplemented generation/round features as current capability.

## Accessibility and motion

- meet documented contrast targets for text, controls, focus, and status;
- preserve meaning without color;
- support reduced motion;
- avoid rapid flicker or celebratory animation;
- test Candidate pair colors for common color-vision deficiencies;
- keep Evidence citations and state labels readable at compact widths.

## Next design deliverables

1. Three original black-and-white double-R symbol families.
2. Tiny-size tests and collision/resemblance review.
3. Wordmark studies using properly licensed type.
4. Semantic palette tested for WCAG contrast and color-vision deficiencies.
5. App icon, favicon, CLI/tray mark, GitHub social preview, and public hero.
6. Product tokens and one real side-by-side Evidence screen prototype.
7. Monochrome, reversed, high-contrast, and reduced-detail variants.
