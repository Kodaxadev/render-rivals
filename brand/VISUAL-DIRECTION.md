# Visual Direction Working Brief

## Positioning

Render Rivals is not an AI website generator and not a subjective design assistant. It is a local-first competitive evaluation harness that creates or accepts contenders, captures comparable evidence, applies gates, and promotes only a materially stronger result.

## Visual personality

- Competitive, not aggressive.
- Analytical, not clinical.
- Technical, not cryptic.
- Premium, not ornamental.
- Energetic in marketing; quiet and legible inside the product.

## Proposed color roles

The final palette remains open, but the interface should use semantic roles:

- **Current / champion:** stable indigo or neutral blue.
- **Challenger:** warm coral or amber.
- **Promoted winner:** green, used only after a verified win.
- **Arena / canvas:** near-black navy and layered slate surfaces.
- **Evidence:** cool neutral text, grids, charts, and annotations.

Avoid making red and green the only way to distinguish contenders or outcomes.

## Logo criteria

The final mark should:

- work at 16–24 px as a CLI, tray, and favicon mark;
- remain recognizable in one color;
- imply comparison, convergence, selection, or promotion;
- avoid looking like an esports team, cryptocurrency, betting product, or game studio;
- avoid generic crossed swords, trophy cups, lightning bolts, shields, and obvious mirrored `R` monograms unless substantially transformed;
- pair cleanly with a simple wordmark.

## Product UI

The application should prioritize evidence and comparison:

- side-by-side and overlay views;
- stable contender colors and labels;
- score provenance and confidence;
- visible gates and invalidation states;
- promotion as an explicit, deliberate action;
- restrained motion limited to state changes and comparison assistance.

## Marketing system

Marketing can use stronger visual rivalry cues than the application:

- opposing light fields;
- split frames and versus compositions;
- bracket or progression diagrams;
- evidence callouts and score deltas;
- champion-to-challenger storytelling;
- before/after assets that maintain fair framing.

## Next design deliverables

1. Three original symbol families in black and white.
2. Wordmark studies using non-proprietary or properly licensed type.
3. A selected palette tested for WCAG contrast and color-vision deficiencies.
4. App icon, favicon, CLI mark, GitHub social preview, and landing-page hero.
5. Product UI tokens and a small comparison-screen prototype.
